📋 Site Requirements
1

Flat Land (Unobstructed)

Open land with minimal shading from trees, buildings, or terrain features. South-facing slope is ideal.

2

Close to Power Source

Proximity to existing electrical grid infrastructure reduces interconnection costs significantly.

3

County/Municipal Incentives

Many counties offer tax abatements, grants, or expedited permitting for solar projects.

4

Feasibility Study

Professional assessment of solar irradiance, soil conditions, and grid capacity before investment.

🏗️ Construction & Development

Site Preparation

  • Operating engineers on-site for oversight
  • Clear and prep site (vegetation removal)
  • GPS surveying for precise module placement
  • Motor scraper machine for grading
  • Civil work - grading hills, soil replacement
  • Remove stumps (stump puller equipment)
  • Pile driver for mounting structure installation

Electrical Installation

  • Electrical workers for all wiring
  • Digging ditches for underground conduit
  • Ring wire installation
  • Inverter connections
  • Grid interconnection

📝 Business Setup Checklist
  • Form LLC - Limited Liability Company for asset protection
  • Get EIN - Employer Identification Number from IRS
  • DUNS Number - Required for government contracts and some financing
  • Business Bank Account - Separate finances for the solar venture
  • Operate Through Unex Energy - Your own energy company handles development and PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)
  • Secure Financing - Initial capital of ~$90,000+ for land and development
  • Insurance - Property, liability, and equipment coverage
  • Permits - County building permits, electrical permits, interconnection agreement
📅 Development Phases
Phase 1: Land Acquisition & Planning
3-6 months
  • Identify suitable land parcels
  • Conduct feasibility study
  • Negotiate land purchase or lease
  • Form LLC and business accounts
  • Apply for permits
Phase 2: Financing & Contracts
2-4 months
  • Secure project financing (loans, investors, or self-funded)
  • Sign Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with utility
  • Contract with solar equipment suppliers
  • Hire construction contractors
Phase 3: Construction
4-8 months
  • Site clearing and preparation
  • Install mounting structures
  • Mount solar panels
  • Electrical installation and wiring
  • Inverter and transformer installation
  • Grid interconnection
Phase 4: Operation & Revenue
25+ years
  • Commission and testing
  • Begin selling power to grid
  • Monthly revenue from PPA
  • Ongoing maintenance (minimal)
  • Reinvest profits into other ventures

💡 Why Solar as the Foundation?
  • Predictable Income: 25-year PPA contracts guarantee stable revenue
  • Low Operating Costs: Minimal maintenance after installation
  • Tax Benefits: Federal ITC (30%), accelerated depreciation, state incentives
  • Appreciating Asset: Land and equipment retain value
  • Scalable: Start with 1 MW, expand as revenue grows
  • Green Legacy: Positive environmental impact for future generations
  • Passive: Frees you to focus on other ventures while income flows
