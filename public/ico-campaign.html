Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about investing in KENO

What makes KENO different from other educational tokens?
KENO is the only platform with a full blockchain simulator, transaction reversal, and revenue-generating PoRV mining. While competitors offer simple "watch videos, earn tokens" models, KENO provides hands-on experience with real blockchain technology and multiple platform rewards through RVT NFT royalties.
How does Proof-of-Residual-Value (PoRV) mining work?
PoRV mining generates real economic value. Enterprise clients pay to submit AI/ML computation tasks. Miners complete these tasks and earn KENO tokens plus RVT NFTs. These NFTs automatically distribute royalties from commercial usage: 70% to NFT holders, 20% burned (deflationary), 10% to treasury.
What is the minimum investment?
The minimum investment is 0.1 BNB (approximately $60 USD). There is no maximum limit during the private sale for whitelisted investors.
Is there a lockup period for tokens?
No! KENO tokens have no vesting or lockup period. You can trade immediately after purchase. This gives you full liquidity and control over your investment.
How do I get whitelisted for the private sale?
Visit our ICO platform and connect your MetaMask wallet. Our team reviews applications and adds approved addresses to the whitelist. Whitelisted investors get 20% bonus tokens and early access to PoRV mining.
What blockchain is KENO built on?
KENO Token is deployed on Binance Smart Chain (BSC) as a BEP-20 token. BSC offers low transaction fees and fast confirmation times, making it ideal for educational use and frequent transactions.
What are the platform utility features for token holders?
Token holders benefit from: (1) PoRV mining rewards, (2) RVT NFT royalties (70% of commercial usage), (3) Deflationary mechanics (20% token burn), (4) Platform growth from B2B subscriptions, exchange fees, and merchant fees.
Can I use KENO tokens on the platform?
Yes! KENO tokens power the entire ecosystem: pay for courses, access premium features, stake for governance voting, trade on the built-in exchange, use for merchant payments, and more. Real utility drives real demand.
